### [Kerven & Rayvhaun's Project:](https://yatsukki.github.io/cart263/Project1/)

This was definitely one of my favourites. I really loved the draggable window and the fact that you could draw on the canvas. Overall, the interactivity was very fun and felt smooth, like everything responded exactly how you expect it to. It was super interesting to see, and I liked the design and the colours. It felt playful and engaging rather than just something you want to look at, which made me want to spend more time interacting with it. Having the cat API was also awesome, but that’s just my personal opinion because I ADORE cats.

The use of P5 for the drawing feature worked really well, and I think it added a creating aspect that made the project REALLY stand out. It kind of turned the whole thing into a little digital playground, which I really love because it gives the user freedom to mess around and create their own experience, instead of just following a fixed path. It’s inspiring because when I am creating something, while I do love adding some sort of narrative to my work and creating purpose, letting the users create their OWN experience and connect with work is engaging and helps to give a lasting impression. And going back tot he cat API, it honestly made the experience seem fresh, but also unpredictable, since every new image added something different. It kept the project from feeling repetitive and made it more dynamic overall.

I also think the draggable window added a really nice touch in terms of interface design. It game it a slightly desktop-like feel, almost like you’re interacting with a mini application instead of just a webpage, and made the experience more immersive.

The code was super neat and easy to read, and even understand. That’s somethinig I really appreciated because it makes the project feel more accessible, especially for someone like me who is still learning. It definitely provided some insight into how I might structure and personalize my own projects in the future, especially when it comes to combining interactivity elements with APIs.

Overall, it felt cohesive and easy to navigate, as well as understand right away. Sometimes people create things that take a bit more time to figure out and understand, so it was really nice to immediately get the vision and just explore without overthinking what I’m supposed to do. It made the whole experience feel intuitive, which I think is an important part of interactive design.

I won't like though, it was very frustrating trying to press the start button. No matter how many times I tried, I could not for the life of me manage to get it. And that was clearly very much the intention. But that didn't stop me from liking it.
